Save Energy

There are numerous energy efficient options for businesses and houses who are looking for greener alternatives. Besides investing in renewable energy sources, there are many practices to save energy or reduce your carbon footprint. Listed below are some of the most common practices as well as their relevent details.

When exiting a room or the house, remember to turn off the lights and your electric appliances not in use. Even keeping a light on for an hour a day can use up a lot of energy over a year. Even turning off the heater when exiting the house can save a lot of energy.

LEDs can last longer and use a lot less energy than traditional light bulbs. According to Energy Star, their LED light bulbs use up to 90% less energy and can last 15 more years than traditional light bulbs.

Some windows can save more energy than others. According to the Department of Energy, heat gain and loss through windows are responsible for 25-30% of residental heating and cooling energy use. Methods such as filling in gaps and installing shades or blinds can help reduce energy use.

Water heating can use up a lot of energy. Hot water is mostly used for showers, washing dishes, and cleaning clothes. Using cold water as much as possible can reduce hot water usage, such as hand-washing dishes and taking shorter or colder showers.
